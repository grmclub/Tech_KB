Books for Algorithmic Trading I Wish I Had Read Sooner 

Book Categories and Recommendations:

    Beginner Books:
        Systematic Trading by Robert Carver (0:43): Highly recommended for its modular framework for trading systems.
        Trading Systems and Methods by Perry Kaufman (1:27): An extensive encyclopedia for reference.
        Advances in Financial Machine Learning by Marcos López de Prado (1:56): A critical read focused on overfitting and modern ML techniques.

    Risk Management:
        The Leverage Space Trading Model by Ralph Vince (2:51): An engaging, story-driven guide to risk management.
        The Mathematics of Money Management by Ralph Vince (3:45): A more technical reference for advanced mathematical concepts.

    Trading Indicators:
        John Ehlers' series (4:22): Books like Rocket Science for Traders, Cybernetic Analysis, and Cycle Analytics focus on digital signal processing and cycle detection.
        Statistically Sound Indicators by Timothy Masters (5:03): Exceptional for learning about stationarity and indicator normalization.

    Trading Strategies:
        Universal Tactics of Successful Trend Trading (5:46), Stocks on the Move (6:03), and Cybernetic Trading Strategies (6:30) cover topics ranging from trend following and relative momentum to genetic algorithms.

    Trading System Development:
        Testing and Tuning Market Trading Systems (7:18) and Permutation and Randomization Tests (7:49), both by Timothy Masters, are essential for rigorous walk-forward testing and Monte Carlo analysis.

    General Knowledge (Useful for Trading):
        Numerical Recipes (8:26), Assessing and Improving Prediction and Classification (8:46), and Data-Driven Science and Engineering (9:14) provide deep insights into optimization, modeling, and dynamical systems.

    Niche Topics:
        Includes Technical Analysis for Algorithmic Pattern Recognition (9:46), Detecting Regime Change in Computational Finance (10:10), and Trading on Sentiment (10:30).
